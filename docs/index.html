The concept

What it actually is

Brain Vault is a structured folder of Markdown files for organizing the parts of life and work that usually scatter across tools. It is intentionally plain: the vault is the asset, Markdown is the format, Git is the history, and optional AI tools are helpers.

The point is not to create another place to hoard documents. The point is to make projects, tasks, journal entries, decisions, ideas, workflows, and reusable knowledge easier to connect, remember, and act on.

What this is not

  • Not a replacement for official systems of record
  • Not a secrets manager or password vault
  • Not a mandate that every personal thought belongs in a work repo
  • Not locked to any AI vendor, model, or note-taking app
  • Not a dumping ground for customer, HR, legal, medical, or regulated records
  • Not useful unless the owner keeps boundaries clean

What this is

  • A connected system for personal life, work, journal, projects, and tasks
  • A business operating system when used by a team
  • A place for reusable knowledge, decisions, solutions, and gotchas
  • A portable folder that can move between tools
  • A practical way to reduce repeated explanation and rediscovery

ai/ Optional assistant guidance for how tools should behave when you choose to use them with the vault.
_private/ Local scratch notes ignored by Git. Useful for drafts, but not secure enough for secrets or regulated data.
Role standpoints

What it means for different users

The same structure creates different value depending on the role. The important shift is that Brain Vault is no longer only a team knowledge repository. It can support individual work, personal organization, shared team memory, and governance-aware AI usage.

Individual user standpoint

Less repeated setup

Your current projects, tasks, journal entries, ideas, and prior decisions live in one structure instead of scattered across apps and memory.

One structure for work and life

You can use the same mental model for work projects, personal goals, routines, learning, and reusable notes without mixing the files themselves.

Better continuity

When you return to something weeks later, the vault gives you a clear path back: what happened, why it mattered, and what remains open.

Portable habits

The structure works with local files, Git, Markdown editors, and optional AI helpers. The tools can change. Your knowledge stays yours.

Team standpoint

Shared operating memory

Runbooks, decisions, project context, meeting notes, and lessons learned become easier to find and reuse.

Faster onboarding

A new team member can inspect how the team works instead of relying only on meetings, chat history, and tribal knowledge.

Reduced dependency on one person

Procedures and explanations that used to live in one person's head can become team-owned knowledge.

Better AI output

When you choose to use AI, the assistant can work from real team context: conventions, known constraints, prior decisions, and reusable workflows.

Security and privacy standpoint

No secrets in files

The vault should contain references to approved secure systems, not passwords, tokens, keys, certificates, or connection strings.

Audience boundaries

Personal-life material does not belong in a team-owned business vault. Company or customer data does not belong in a personal vault unless policy explicitly allows it.

Local private is not secure private

_private/ is ignored by Git, but it is not encrypted or governed. It is for scratch thinking, not secrets or regulated records.

Human approval remains required

AI can draft, analyze, and propose. Consequential business actions still require human review and the official approval path.

Governance standpoint

Git-backed history

Changes are inspectable over time. That helps with recovery, review, and understanding how the vault evolved.

Not the system of record

Brain Vault complements tools such as ticketing, document management, compliance systems, and production logging. It should link to official records instead of replacing them.

Configurable operating contract

AGENTS.md tells assistants how to behave in that vault: what to scan, what to protect, where content belongs, and when to ask.

Policy can be built into the workflow

Team rules, naming conventions, known risks, and review expectations can live beside the work instead of being remembered informally.

Common questions

Objections and honest answers

The mixed personal-and-work model is useful only if the boundaries are explicit. These are the questions that should be answered before broader adoption.

"Does this mean personal content goes into a company repository?"
No. A team-owned business vault should not contain personal-life journals, household information, private family notes, health notes, or unrelated personal material. Brain Vault supports personal use and business use, but the intended audience determines where the content belongs.
"Can I use the same structure for personal and work?"
Yes. That is one of the reasons for the new design. You can use the same patterns for projects, tasks, decisions, notes, and reusable knowledge while keeping separate folders or separate vaults for different audiences.
"Is Brain Vault a system of record?"
No. It is a context and working-knowledge layer. Tickets, approvals, HR records, legal records, customer records, finance records, compliance evidence, and production audit logs stay in the approved systems of record. The vault can link to them and summarize safe context around them.
"What about privacy and management visibility?"
Visibility depends on where the vault lives and who has access. A private personal vault is different from a shared business vault. A leadership summary is different from raw note access. The system should be deployed with clear rules about audience, ownership, and what belongs in each location.
"Does this create dependency on one AI vendor?"
No. Brain Vault is built from local Markdown files. You can use it with ChatGPT, Claude Code, Codex, Copilot, OpenCode, local models, Obsidian, VS Code, or no AI at all. The vault itself lives in files you control, and you choose what context to share with any assistant.
"Will people stop thinking and blindly follow AI?"
That risk is real with any AI-assisted workflow. Brain Vault does not remove human responsibility. The healthier pattern is to use the assistant to explain, draft, compare, document, and propose while humans validate consequential actions and keep official approvals intact.
"What should never go in the vault?"
Secrets, credentials, private keys, tokens, recovery codes, regulated data, raw customer records, HR records, legal records, financial records, medical records, and any material that the intended audience should not be able to read.

El concepto

Qué es realmente

Brain Vault es una carpeta estructurada de archivos Markdown para organizar las partes de la vida y el trabajo que normalmente quedan dispersas entre herramientas. Es intencionalmente simple: el vault es el activo, Markdown es el formato, Git es el historial y las herramientas de IA son ayudas opcionales.

La idea no es crear otro lugar para acumular documentos. La idea es hacer que proyectos, tareas, entradas del diario, decisiones, ideas, flujos de trabajo y conocimiento reutilizable sean más fáciles de conectar, recordar y convertir en acción.

Qué no es

  • No reemplaza los sistemas oficiales de registro
  • No es un gestor de secretos ni un password vault
  • No es una obligación de poner cada pensamiento personal en un repositorio de trabajo
  • No está amarrado a ningún proveedor de IA, modelo o app de notas
  • No es un lugar para volcar datos de clientes, HR, legales, médicos o regulados
  • No funciona bien si el dueño no mantiene límites claros

Qué sí es

  • Un sistema conectado para vida personal, trabajo, diario, proyectos y tareas
  • Un sistema operativo de negocio cuando lo usa un equipo
  • Un lugar para conocimiento, decisiones, soluciones y gotchas reutilizables
  • Una carpeta portátil que puede moverse entre herramientas
  • Una forma práctica de reducir explicación repetida y redescubrimiento

Preguntas comunes

Objeciones y respuestas honestas

El modelo mixto de personal y trabajo solo es útil si los límites son explícitos. Estas son las preguntas que conviene responder antes de una adopción más amplia.

"¿Esto significa que el contenido personal va en un repositorio de la compañía?"
No. Un vault business propiedad del equipo no debe contener diarios personales, información del hogar, notas privadas de familia, notas de salud ni material personal no relacionado. Brain Vault soporta uso personal y uso laboral, pero el público previsto determina dónde pertenece el contenido.
"¿Puedo usar la misma estructura para lo personal y lo laboral?"
Sí. Esa es una de las razones del nuevo diseño. Puedes usar los mismos patrones para proyectos, tareas, decisiones, notas y conocimiento reutilizable mientras mantienes carpetas o vaults separados para distintos públicos.
"¿Brain Vault es un sistema oficial de registro?"
No. Es una capa de contexto y conocimiento de trabajo. Tickets, aprobaciones, registros de HR, documentos legales, registros de clientes, finanzas, evidencia de cumplimiento y logs de producción se quedan en los sistemas aprobados. El vault puede enlazarlos y resumir contexto seguro alrededor de ellos.
"¿Qué pasa con la privacidad y la visibilidad de management?"
La visibilidad depende de dónde vive el vault y quién tiene acceso. Un vault personal privado es distinto a un vault business compartido. Un resumen para liderazgo es distinto al acceso a notas crudas. El sistema debe desplegarse con reglas claras sobre audiencia, propiedad y qué pertenece en cada lugar.
"¿Esto crea dependencia de un solo proveedor de IA?"
No. Brain Vault está construido con archivos Markdown locales. Puedes usarlo con ChatGPT, Claude Code, Codex, Copilot, OpenCode, modelos locales, Obsidian, VS Code o sin IA. El vault vive en archivos que tú controlas, y tú eliges qué contexto compartir con cualquier asistente.
"¿La gente dejará de pensar y seguirá a la IA ciegamente?"
Ese riesgo es real en cualquier flujo asistido por IA. Brain Vault no elimina la responsabilidad humana. El patrón sano es usar el asistente para explicar, redactar, comparar, documentar y proponer, mientras las personas validan las acciones importantes y mantienen las aprobaciones oficiales.
"¿Qué nunca debe ir en el vault?"
Secretos, credenciales, llaves privadas, tokens, códigos de recuperación, datos regulados, registros crudos de clientes, registros de HR, documentos legales, registros financieros, registros médicos y cualquier material que el público previsto no deba poder leer.
