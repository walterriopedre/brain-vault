# Personal Vault Guide

Use a personal Brain Vault to organize private projects, learning, routines, creative work, and
life admin.

---

## Recommended Starting Structure

Start with:

- `personal/` for personal-mode notes.
- `personal/projects/` for active personal projects.
- `personal/tasks/` for personal next actions.
- `personal/routines/` for recurring routines.
- `personal/notes/` for general notes.
- `RAW/` for captured articles, YouTube notes, PDFs, transcripts, and audio messages before processing.
- `shared/knowledge/` for reusable understanding.
- `_private/` for local scratch notes.

Do not overbuild the structure at the beginning. Add folders when repeated use makes the need
obvious.

---

## Good First Notes

- A project you are actively working on.
- A routine you repeat weekly.
- A learning topic you want to track.
- A decision you keep revisiting.
- A gotcha you do not want to rediscover.

---

## Suggested Templates

- `templates/personal/personal-project.md`
- `templates/personal/routine.md`
- `templates/shared/note.md`
- `templates/shared/task.md`
- `templates/shared/decision.md`

---

## Privacy Guidance

Use a private repository or local-only folder for real personal content.

Avoid storing highly sensitive information unless you have the right security model. Brain Vault is
not a password manager, medical record system, financial vault, or legal archive.

---

## AI Guidance

Ask the assistant to read relevant notes before helping. Good prompts:

```text
Read my project note and open tasks, then help me choose the next three actions.
```

```text
Turn these notes into a reusable checklist. Flag anything that should stay private.
```
